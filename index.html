This project page provides additional qualitative results, benchmark details, and resources for our face swapping survey and CASIA FaceSwapping benchmark.

Overview

Face swapping aims to transfer the identity of a source face onto a target while preserving target-specific attributes such as pose, expression, illumination, and background. This project accompanies our survey and benchmark paper, which provides a unified taxonomy of existing methods and introduces CASIA FaceSwapping, a high-quality benchmark designed for fair and fine-grained evaluation.

Our benchmark emphasizes three practically important aspects: baseline performance under normal conditions, cross-ethnicity generalization, and robustness to attribute variations. We also provide additional visual comparisons to complement the quantitative analysis in the paper.

Benchmark Overview

CASIA FaceSwapping

  • 1,291 subjects and 2,582 videos
  • 4K resolution (2160 × 3840)
  • Balanced demographic composition
  • Controlled pose, expression, and illumination variations
  • Designed specifically for face swapping evaluation

Protocol Goal Setting Pairs

Cross-attribute Robustness to attribute shifts Same ethnicity, varying pose / expression / illumination 4,300

Representative Results

Quantitative evaluation shows that different methods exhibit distinct trade-offs between identity fidelity, attribute consistency, and visual realism. Our benchmark highlights that performance under standard settings does not necessarily translate to stronger robustness under cross-ethnicity or cross-attribute scenarios.
