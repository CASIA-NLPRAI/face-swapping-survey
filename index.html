Overview

Face swapping has advanced rapidly with 3DMM-based models, autoencoders, GANs, StyleGAN, and diffusion models. However, existing methods remain fragmented across paradigms, and evaluation is often inconsistent due to heterogeneous datasets and protocols. This project aims to provide both a unified methodological perspective and a standardized benchmark for controlled analysis.

Unified Survey

We organize representative methods into five major paradigms and discuss their design principles, strengths, and limitations from a unified perspective.

New Benchmark

We introduce CASIA FaceSwapping, a high-quality benchmark with 4K videos, balanced demographic composition, and explicit attribute variations.

Standardized Evaluation

We establish Normal, Cross-ethnicity, and Cross-attribute protocols to enable fair and interpretable comparison across methods.

CASIA FaceSwapping Benchmark

CASIA FaceSwapping is designed specifically for evaluating face swapping methods under realistic and controlled conditions. It emphasizes high visual quality, balanced demographic coverage, and fine-grained attribute variations.

Benchmark Highlights

  • Balanced demographic distribution across Asian, African, and Caucasian subjects.
  • Two recordings per identity: one normal and one with attribute variation.
  • Controlled pose, expression, and illumination changes for robust evaluation.
  • Aligned face images and multiple video quality settings for realistic deployment scenarios.

Evaluation Protocols

We design three protocols to isolate distinct factors and enable targeted analysis of baseline performance, cross-group generalization, and robustness under dynamic attributes.

Protocol 1: Normal Source and target pairs are selected from normal recordings within the same ethnic group. This protocol measures baseline identity transfer and attribute preservation under standard conditions.
Protocol 2: Cross-ethnicity Source and target subjects belong to different ethnic groups while other factors remain controlled. This protocol evaluates demographic generalization and fairness.
Protocol 3: Cross-attribute Source and target pairs involve dynamic changes in pose, expression, or illumination. This protocol tests robustness and attribute consistency under more challenging conditions.
